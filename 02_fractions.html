Proposition 1.

Given vulgar fraction \(\large\frac{a}{b} \in \mathbb{Q}\) where \(1 < a < b\) and \((a,b) = 1\).

Then \(\exists\) some \(0 < x < y \in \mathbb{N}\) where \((x,y) = 1\) and \(x \le a\), \(y \le b\)

which satisfy the equation \(ay - bx = 1\), and where \(\large\frac{x}{y} \normalsize < \large\frac{a}{b}\).

Clearly,

\(\large\frac{a}{b} \normalsize = \large\frac{x}{y} \normalsize +\) \(\large\frac{a}{b} \normalsize - \large\frac{a}{b}\) \(\Longrightarrow\) \(\large\frac{a}{b} \normalsize = \large\frac{x}{y} \normalsize +\) \(\large\frac{ay-bx}{by} \normalsize\)
